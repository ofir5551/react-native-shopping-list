## Design Context

### Users
Initially personal use and close family sharing household shopping tasks. Eventually a public product. Users are in the moment — standing in a grocery store, rushing before leaving home. The job is fast, friction-free list management: add items quickly, check them off, share with a partner. Speed and reliability matter more than aesthetics.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: Calm, Warm, Focused.**

Friendly without being cutesy. Reliable without being cold. Feels like a well-made everyday object — not flashy, just solid and pleasant to use every day. Think a good kitchen tool: functional design with just enough warmth to feel inviting.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference apps**: Listonic (clean task-focused lists, good information density, no clutter), Google Keep (friendly card-based layout, approachable, fast to scan)
- **Tone**: Functional first. Warmth comes from color temperature and typography, not decoration. No animations for animation's sake.
- **Color**: Warm-neutral base (already established: cream `#f7f4f0`, forest green `#1f7a5a`). The green is a strong brand anchor — keep it central.
- **Anti-references**: Flashy productivity apps, neon/dark-mode-first designs, anything that feels like it's trying to be impressive rather than useful.
- **Theme**: Light + dark both supported and polished.

### Design Principles
1. **Speed over style** — every interaction should feel instant. Optimistic UI, minimal chrome, no decorative friction.
2. **Warmth through restraint** — personality comes from color temperature, rounded corners, and micro-copy, not decoration or illustration.
3. **Scannable at a glance** — list items are the hero. Typography and spacing should make a list of 15 items easy to parse, not beautiful to photograph.
4. **Trustworthy defaults** — new users should feel oriented immediately. Nothing hidden, nothing surprising. Discoverability over power-user density.
5. **Functional beauty** — if a design choice doesn't make the app easier or faster to use, cut it.
